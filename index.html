I am a Research Scientist at IBM Quantum (Thomas J. Watson Research Center), working on the Quantum Algorithms for Near-Term and Early Fault-Tolerance team. My research focuses on quantum algorithms for physics simulation, thermal state preparation, quantum complexity of noisy systems, and measurement-based quantum algorithms.

I received my Ph.D. in Physics from MIT (2019), advised by Seth Lloyd and Leonid Levitov. Before IBM, I was a Postdoctoral Fellow at QuICS & JQI (University of Maryland College Park).

Research

Quantum Algorithms & Simulation

I develop quantum algorithms for physics simulation on near-term and early fault-tolerant hardware, including tensor-network-enhanced Hamiltonian simulation and efficient quantum Gibbs sampling protocols. A key result: designing and executing quantum experiments on up to 124 qubits.

Nature Comm. 2025 PRX Quantum 2025 arXiv 2025

Quantum Complexity & Noise

I study the computational power of noisy quantum systems. A central result: circuits with nonunital noise are computationally universal with only polylogarithmic overhead — realistic noise doesn't necessarily kill quantum advantage.

PRX Quantum 2025 PRX Quantum 2022 PRX Quantum 2021

Topological Quantum Matter and Physics of Boundaries

My work on topological phases combines theoretical predictions with quantum simulation, including observing and braiding Majorana modes on programmable quantum hardware and establishing stability of Floquet topological phases under disorder.

Nature Comm. 2023 PRL 2020 PRL 2018 Nature Physics 2016 PNAS 2018

Autonomous Quantum Error Correction

I investigate fundamental limits of passive quantum error correction using engineered dissipation, establishing bounds on how well autonomous decoders can protect quantum information without active measurement.

Quantum 2025
